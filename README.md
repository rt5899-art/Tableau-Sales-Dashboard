# Tableau-Sales-Dashboard📊 Tableau Sales Dashboard
An end-to-end Sales Analytics project built in Tableau, designed to give businesses a clear view of sales performance, profit trends, customer segments, and regional distribution — all through interactive, filterable visualizations.

📌 Project Overview
This project transforms raw sales data into a dynamic Tableau dashboard that answers critical business questions:

Which categories and sub-categories are driving the most revenue and profit?
How do sales and profit trends evolve month over month and year over year?
Which customer segments and regions perform best?
Where are the losses — and what's causing them?

The dashboard is designed for business analysts, sales managers, and decision-makers who need quick, visual access to performance metrics.

📁 Repository Structure
Tableau-Sales-Dashboard/
│
├── [Sales Dashboard].twbx     # Packaged Tableau workbook (data + visuals)
└── README.md                  # Project documentation

.twbx is a packaged workbook — it bundles the data source and all dashboard sheets into a single file, so no external data connection is needed.


📊 Dashboard Overview
🔑 Key KPIs
MetricDescriptionTotal SalesOverall revenue across all ordersTotal ProfitNet profit after costsProfit MarginProfit as a percentage of salesTotal OrdersCount of distinct ordersQuantity SoldTotal units sold

📉 Charts & Visuals
1. Sales & Profit Trend (Line Chart)

Month-over-month and year-over-year performance
Identifies seasonal spikes and downturns

2. Sales by Category & Sub-Category (Bar Chart)

Breaks down revenue across product categories (e.g., Technology, Furniture, Office Supplies)
Highlights top and bottom performers by sub-category

3. Profit by Sub-Category (Bar Chart)

Reveals which sub-categories are profitable vs. loss-making
Essential for pricing and inventory decisions

4. Sales by Region (Map / Bar Chart)

Geographic breakdown of sales performance
Identifies high-opportunity and underperforming regions

5. Customer Segment Analysis (Donut / Bar Chart)

Compares Consumer, Corporate, and Home Office segments
Helps tailor marketing and sales strategies

6. Sales vs. Profit Scatter Plot

Plots individual orders to uncover discount-driven losses
Highlights outliers and high-discount, low-profit zones

7. Year-over-Year Growth Comparison

Side-by-side comparison of current vs. prior year performance
Tracks growth trajectory across all key metrics


🔍 Filters & Interactivity
The dashboard supports dynamic filtering by:

Year / Date Range — drill into specific time periods
Region — focus on East, West, Central, or South
Category — isolate Technology, Furniture, or Office Supplies
Customer Segment — slice by Consumer, Corporate, or Home Office

All visuals cross-filter each other — clicking any chart automatically updates the rest of the dashboard.

🛠️ Tools Used
ToolPurposeTableau Desktop / Tableau PublicDashboard design and interactive visualizationExcel / CSVUnderlying sales dataset

🚀 Getting Started
Prerequisites

Tableau Public (free) or Tableau Desktop (licensed)

Steps

Clone the repository

bash   git clone https://github.com/rt5899-art/Tableau-Sales-Dashboard.git
   cd Tableau-Sales-Dashboard

Open the workbook

   Double-click the .twbx file — it opens directly in Tableau Desktop or Tableau Public

Explore the dashboard

Use the filter panel on the right/top to slice the data
Click any bar, region, or data point to cross-filter all charts
Hover over marks for detailed tooltips

💡 Key Insights

Technology is typically the highest revenue category, while Office Supplies leads on profit margin.
Furniture often shows thin or negative profit margins — particularly in the Tables sub-category — due to heavy discounting.
The Consumer segment drives the highest order volume, but Corporate customers tend to have higher average order values.
Q4 (October–December) consistently shows the strongest sales performance, driven by seasonal demand.
The West region frequently leads in total sales, while the Central region shows higher loss rates.
High discount rates (above 40%) strongly correlate with negative profit — a key area for pricing strategy review.


📚 Data Source
This project uses the Sample Superstore dataset — a widely used retail dataset provided by Tableau, containing orders, returns, and people data across US regions. It includes fields such as Order Date, Ship Mode, Customer Segment, Product Category, Sales, Quantity, Discount, and Profit.
