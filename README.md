## Sales Performance

### Project Overview

This project features an interactive Tableau Sales Dashboard designed to track, analyze, and optimize global sales performance. By consolidating key financial and operational metrics, the dashboard provides stakeholders with deep visibility into revenue trends, profitability drivers, order volumes, and regional performance across a multi-year timeline (2015–2017).

### Tools & Technologies

Business Intelligence: Tableau Desktop

Data Sources: Sales, Cost, and Order transactional datasets

Analytics Techniques: Time-series forecasting, cohort analysis, moving averages, and multi-axis data visualization

### Key Business Insights
 
* High-Level Financials: Maintained strong financial health with total Revenue of ₹24.91M, total Profit of ₹10.46M, and operational Costs of ₹14.46M.

* Order Fulfillment: Successfully processed 25,164 Total Orders, moving an aggregate Quantity of 84,174 units.

* Quality Control: Kept product returns low with a Return Quantity of 1,828 units, indicating high customer satisfaction and accurate fulfillment.

* Accelerated Growth: The YTD-QTD-MTD Running Total chart reveals a clear upward inflection point in sales velocity starting around Q3 2016, with growth accelerating consistently into mid-     2017.

* Regional Profit Concentrates: Two primary markets dominate international profitability—the United States accounts for 32.17% of profit, closely followed by Australia at 29.42%. Together,     they generate over 60% of global profits.

* Emerging vs. Lagging Markets: The United Kingdom contributes 11.62% of profit, while Canada lags significantly at 7.25%, signaling an underperformance in North American market penetration    relative to the US.

### Strategic Recommendations

Insight: The US and Australia drive a combined 61.59% of global profit.

* Action: Allocate additional marketing spend and inventory priority to these high-margin corridors to maximize ROI during peak seasons.

Insight: Sales velocity experienced an aggressive surge starting in Q3 2016.

* Action: Audit marketing campaigns, product updates, or client-acquisition strategies implemented during Q3 2016 to standardize and replicate these growth drivers across underperforming periods.

Insight: Canada contributes only 7.25% to overall profit, drastically trailing the neighboring US market.

* Action: Conduct a localized market-fit study in Canada to identify supply chain bottlenecks, pricing mismatches, or gaps in product awareness.

Insight: A stable moving average line reveals minor short-term profit volatility despite rising overall revenue.

* Action: Introduce dynamic inventory controls and optimized logistics scheduling to stabilize operational costs during sudden, high-volume order spikes.
